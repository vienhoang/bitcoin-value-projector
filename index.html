Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Bitcoin investing and using this calculator

What is CAGR?
CAGR stands for Compound Annual Growth Rate. It represents the mean annual growth rate of an investment over a specified time period longer than one year. It's one of the most accurate ways to calculate returns for anything that can rise or fall in value over time.
What is DCA (Dollar-Cost Averaging)?
DCA is an investment strategy where you invest a fixed amount of money at regular intervals (e.g., monthly), regardless of the asset's price. This approach helps reduce the impact of volatility and removes the pressure of trying to "time the market." Many Bitcoiners use DCA to build their stack over time.
Is this calculator accurate?
This calculator provides mathematical projections based on the CAGR you input. However, bitcoin's price is highly volatile and past performance does not guarantee future results. Use these projections as a planning tool, not as financial advice. Always do your own research and never invest more than you can afford to lose.
Is my data private?
Yes! This calculator runs entirely in your browser. No data is sent to any server, stored in any database, or shared with anyone. Everything you enter stays on your device. You can even use this calculator offline after the initial page load.
What's the difference between the scenarios?
Conservative (15% CAGR) reflects slower, steadier growth. Moderate (25% CAGR) is closer to bitcoin's historical average over certain periods. Bullish (40% CAGR) represents more aggressive growth scenarios. You can also input your own custom CAGR based on your personal outlook.
Should I invest in Bitcoin?
This tool is for educational and planning purposes only, not financial advice. Whether you should invest in Bitcoin depends on your personal financial situation, risk tolerance, and investment goals. Consider consulting with a qualified financial advisor before making any investment decisions.
Why does Bitcoin only have 21 million coins?
Bitcoin's 21 million coin limit is hardcoded into its protocol by its creator, Satoshi Nakamoto. This fixed supply makes bitcoin scarce, similar to gold. Unlike fiat currencies that can be printed infinitely, bitcoin's scarcity is guaranteed by mathematics and consensus, making it resistant to inflation.
What is "not your keys, not your coins"?
This is a core principle in Bitcoin: if you don't control the private keys to your bitcoin, you don't truly own it. When bitcoin is held on an exchange, the exchange controls the keys. For true ownership and security, you should withdraw your bitcoin to a wallet where you control the private keys (preferably a hardware wallet).
